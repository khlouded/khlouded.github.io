The Cosmic

scroll down

These monsters represent a variety of concerns that there are beings and monsters beyond comprehension and reality. These monsters cause existential terror and the dread of existence. and interdimensional beings beyond reality and the universe. These monsters were larger than anything imaginable and sometimes broke people minds who have seen them or have interacted with them. These stories of cosmic monsters are driven by the ever prevailing insignificance that humans feel in the vast and large cosmos, where humans live on a planet much smaller than the far reaches of the galaxies. Many of these monsters are created by the work of Howard P. Lovecraft, who created the basis of existential terror. Some of these monsters only exist in the consciousness of the human body, while other have no fixed form or substance and are known by their influence and impact on their interaction with the inorganic and organic material around them. These monsters can have a great variety of tentacles or even plantlike bodies and may even resemble just blobs of goop that do not have any form or recognizable shape, They may be depicted as larger than the earth itself or in the shape of a planet, which exists as an entire organism working together. Some of these monsters are invisible and cause massive destruction in the places they visit. Looking at the bigger picture, cosmic horror in general makes us aware of our fears of the unknowable. We feel small, inconsequential, and totally helpless against these phenomena. These monsters are comparable to a tsunami or an earthquake except they are personified and vilified. Cosmic horror describes how we were never in control and makes people confront their lack of power and influence in the face of these monsters. A lot of cosmic horror tend to make people doubt reality and looks at the unknown elements of the mind and perception. This horror can be defined by even just an inkling or a compulsion that makes people do certain actions or change into certain things. The monster is in the mind and no matter what we do, we cannot get rid of it. Uzumaki is a manga series by Junji Ito that emphasizes this fear of compulsion through the representation of spirals.
